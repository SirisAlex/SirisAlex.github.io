About me

My name is Alex Lawrence. I am a Game Designer with 2-3 years experience.

I am Passionate about making experiences that anyone can enjoy, and I strive to always be improving.

Moving forward, step by step.

I have experience making games as well as interactive experiences. I am proficient with the Unity and Unreal engines, and also with C# and C++ code. I also have experience with designing workflows in Figma and assets in photoshop, with some modeling experience in Maya.

Whenever I am not programming or gaming, I enjoy writing stories and thinking up new characters. I particularly enjoy the process of designing cool future scenarios for potential projects. You will always find new ideas banging around in my head.

Projects

Syllo
Syllo is a game created in one week for the Brackeys Game Jam 2025 on Itch.io. I was lead developer of the game and worked around the clock to design layouts, implement player interaction, and guide the player through their tasks to reach the end of the game. In just one week, we managed to implement three days of story-driven content, 10-20 unique task designs, and 15 emails full of juicy dialogue. Itch Page for Syllo

Wilderwood
Wilderwood is a 3D collectathon made in Unity by me and a group of friends. As lead UI Designer/Developer, I was in charge of prototyping and refining the look and feel of the our interface. The design is simple and rustic looking, which I thought best fit the elements of our casual experience, and after multiple iterations, I streamlined the design to give the player the information they needed without being too crowded and messy.

Doots Never Die
Doots Never Die is a game made in Unity in fall 2021 by me and a team of RIT students. The game is a top-down shooter where you're fighting against the clock to scare as many kids as possible on Halloween night. The game was programmed in Unity and published with WebGL. My role in Doots Never Die was on the enemy AI of the children in the game. I had to figure out how the enemies would react to the player's position at all times and carefully balance their speed and aggressiveness to make the game challenging, but fair. WebGL build of the game

Submerged Submerged is a Unity game designed in spring 2021 by me and a team of developers. The game is a physics-based platformer taking place entirely underwater. I was a lead level designer and programmer for the game, determining the flow of player movement and designing some of the more interesting mechanics of our grappling hook, such as the slingshot and hanging onto moving objects. Independent build of the game
